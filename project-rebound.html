Project Rebound Background

Project Rebound is a program that assists formerly incarcerated individuals in succeeding in higher education at California State Universities. I was a part of Aakash's lab at San Francisco State University, where I collaborated with team members on supporting digital literacy for returning citizens.

The Problem

In today's rapidly evolving job market, having digital skills is crucial. Yet, for returning citizens, there's an obstacle: limited digital knowledge. Not having digital skills makes it harder to succeed in college and to get job opportunities.

The Objective

The goal of this project is to assist returning citizens in learning digital skills, thereby enhancing their success in college and expanding their job opportunities.

The Challenge

Helping returning citizens learn digital skills isn't clear and simple. Just having things like a Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) or a forum won't be enough to fix the issue. How can we find the most appropriate solutions that address the specific needs and challenges of this group of people?

How Might We

Based on the initial research, we discovered that students had different levels of familiarity with technology. While some were comfortable searching for information online independently, most struggled with technical terminology. Another concern was that when staff members were unavailable, students had few other people to ask for help. As a result, providing support has been identified as the second priority. Therefore, we have prioritized the following problems:

P0: Trouble with Tech Words

How might we simplify technical terms to make them more understandable for students?

P1: Lack of Support When Needed

How might we support students when Project Rebound staff members are not available?

P3: Differing Skill Levels

How might we create tailored learning pathways to accommodate students with varying tech proficiency?

P4: Difficulty Articulating Problems

How might we provide tools to help students share their problems more effectively?

P5: Hesitation in Seeking Help

How might we encourage students to seek help without hesitation?

- The contents are carefully curated by the staff of Project Rebound to match the students' level of understanding.

- Improving the visual layout to assist students in locating their needs.

- Adjusted to students' preferences, it enables audio questions with screen captures, prompt responses from online staff, Zoom for assistance, and access to a FAQ section for common queries.

Project Rebound students use the Personal Learning Path to address their learning needs, enhance their skills, and improve their outcomes.

- By conducting assessments to gauge users' technological proficiency, a personalized course is crafted to match their skill level.

‚ú® Reflection

Outcome Discussion

In today's fast-changing job market, having basic digital skills is vital, especially for those rejoining society after incarceration. Our project aims to close the digital gap and provide essential support to each Project Rebound student, helping them acquire crucial digital skills.

Empathize With User

Empathy with the user is a fundamental principle for UX designers. It is especially important in this project, as the user group has unique needs and challenges. To create a tool that assists them, it is essential to be a good listener and observer, deeply connecting with their pains and difficulties.

Insights That Informed Design Decisions

Insights are not a one-time affair, they continue to hold value throughout the design process. They act as a guiding compass, ensuring we stay on the right track. Regular reflection on these insights is crucial to assess whether the design effectively addresses the identified problems.
