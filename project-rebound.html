Project Rebound Brief

Project Rebound is a program that supports the higher education and successful reintegration of formerly incarcerated individuals wishing to enroll and succeed at the California State University.

I was a part of Aakash's lab at SFSU, where I collaborated with team members on supporting digital literacy for returning citizens. Our work involved conducting user research, defining problems, creating wireframes, developing high-fidelity versions, and conducting usability tests.These activities aimed to ensure that the solutions we designed effectively addressed the population's specific needs and challenges.

I had an amazing time working with my teammates as we conducted interviews with people from Project Rebound, including both staff and students to gain a deep understanding of the challenges that they face.

📌 Context

Why is this important

Each year, thousands of individuals are released from prisons, but they face higher unemployment rates compared to the national average (Assistant Secretary for Planning and Evaluation).

Limited digital literacy due to resource constraints poses a challenge for formerly incarcerated citizens in today's job market. When starting college, they encounter difficulties with technical terminology, accountability, assignments, and seeking assistance. They also face vulnerabilities to online scams. Insufficient support leads to low retention rates and employment struggles, hindering their successful re-integration into society.

Challenge

When people can't articulate the problem they are having, how will they search? And how can we support them to ask for help?

🔎 Research

Research Method

Used qualitative methods - interviews to gauge a better understanding of how we could support students in asking for help regarding technological issues.

Conducted interviews with Project Rebound students

At an early stage, two teammates and I interviewed 6 Project Rebound students to directly gather the students' perspectives, understand their challenges, and identify needs when it comes to technological issues.

Conducted interviews with Project Rebound staff members

We conducted individual interviews with 3 Project Rebound staff members to get their input on how to support students. Their experience working with students provided valuable knowledge about the challenges students face and the areas where they require support.

Research Goal

The primary goal of this study is to investigate Project Rebound students' use of technology and identify potential areas for improvement and support.

Analyzing Data - Interview Main Takeaways

Following the interviews, we summarized each interview, capturing the important points. From the summaries, we identified codes that formed the basis of our affinity diagram in Miro. Later, we organized each point to reveal relationships such as dependencies and sequences, which enabled us to identify themes.

Limited Resources

Project Rebound students face significant challenges due to financial constraints and limited resources.

Support from Project Rebound Staff

Project Rebound staff invest tremendous effort in assisting students when students need help.

Different tech comfort levels

Project Rebound students vary in tech comfort. Some struggle with technical terms and expressing issues.

Student Potential and Aspirations

Project Rebound students have skills, and they want to make a positive impact on the community.

⚡️ Problem Identification

Problem Diagnosis

Based on the research, we identified four key design challenges to address.

Problem Articulation

How can we assist students who face difficulties in articulating their problems?

Different Technology Comfort Levels

How can resources accommodate the diverse needs of different students?

Technological Terminology

How can we address the issue of unfamiliar technical jargon faced by students?

Staff Availability and Support

What are the options for supporting students when staff members are not available?

Spectrums And Situations Framework

Tackle The Problems

During the process, it's crucial to remember that we are not the users. Drawing from the ideations section, I outlined the situations and proposed strategies to address each problem.

The ideas are designed to assist users in various situations and are interconnected. By giving students chances to learn technical skills and understand tech terms, we empower them to better express their problems. This approach ensures that users not only gain knowledge but also learn how to communicate their challenges.

⭐️ Usability Tests

Evaluations

To assess the effectiveness and usability of the design, we conducted usability testing, gathering valuable feedback and insights.

Internal Test

Internal critiques for initial feedback using Nielsen Norman Group's 10 Usability Heuristics for User Interface Design as guidelines.

Usability Tests

We tested the website with Project Rebound users, watching them complete tasks and observing how they interacted with it.

Areas for Improvement

With the final test, participants had positive impressions of the prototypes overall. However,three major areas were identified for improvement based on their feedback.

Add “Common Words”

Users suggested adding "Common Words" to make the feature more useful.

Create a Dropdown Navigation Bar

To enhance navigation, we replaced the navigation bar with a dropdown menu, improving accessibility and making it easier for users to find and access the homepage and sub-pages.

Adding Progress Indicators

Enhance test navigation by adding progress indicators and question navigation buttons. Users can track their progress and easily move between questions.

✨ Reflection

Outcome Discussion

In today's fast-changing job market, having basic digital skills is vital, especially for those rejoining society after incarceration. Our project aims to close the digital gap and provide essential support to each Project Rebound student, helping them acquire crucial digital skills.

Empathize With User

Empathy with the user is a fundamental principle for UX designers. It is especially important in this project, as the user group has unique needs and challenges. To create a tool that assists them, it is essential to be a good listener and observer, deeply connecting with their pains and difficulties.

Insights That Informed Design Decisions

Insights are not a one-time affair, they continue to hold value throughout the design process. They act as a guiding compass, ensuring we stay on the right track. Regular reflection on these insights is crucial to assess whether the design effectively addresses the identified problems.
